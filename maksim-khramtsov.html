Career and Professional Life

Maksim Khramtsov’s parents enrolled him in Karate because they did not know about Taekwondo. But later on, he switched to Taekwondo as he always wanted to compete at the Olympics.

Maksim was able to win the Gold Medal in the men’s 80kg Taekwondo after defeating Jordan’s Saleh Elsharabaty 20-9 in the final match at the ongoing Tokyo Summer Olympics 2020.

He was able to become the first person from Russia to win a gold medal in Taekwondo.

According to the president of Russia, this triumph is a huge win for the athlete as well as his coaches, and also everyone who helped him achieve his long-awaited ambition.

Likewise, Russian President Vladimir Putin himself congratulated Maksim on winning the gold medal and shared his happiness. Maksim also won the gold medal in the 2017 World Taekwondo Championships.

Furthermore, Maksim has won gold medals in the 2017 World Taekwondo Grand Slam, 2018 World Taekwondo Grand Slam, 2018 World Taekwondo Grand Prix, and 2019 World Taekwondo Grand Prix.

He has also won the 2018 European Taekwondo Championships, 2021 European Taekwondo Championships, 2019 Military World Games, and 2015 European Junior Championships.

He also won silver medals in 2017, 2018, and 2019 World Taekwondo Grand Prix. Moreover, Maksim also won a Bronze medal in the 2015 World Taekwondo Grand Prix.
