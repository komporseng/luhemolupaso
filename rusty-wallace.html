Career

After joining NASCAR and competing in Florida races, Rusty Wallace started his professional career in 1979. He ultimately came in third in the standings behind Bay Darnell and A.J. Foyt and won the USAC Stock Car Rookie of the Year honor.

He defeated notable players like Mark Martin, Dickie Trickle, Alan Kulwicki, and Martin Mark to win the American Speed Association Championship later that year, in 1983. Later, in 1980, he started his NASCAR driving career. He came in second in his debut NASCAR race, the 1980 Atlanta 500.

Up until 1988, he continued to advance in his career, taking home six victories in Rockingham, Riverside, Charlotte, Michigan, and Atlanta. In 1989, he captured the NASCAR WINSTON Cup championship. He tried his hand at broadcasting and worked for ABC after retiring from racing.

In addition to making an appearance in the movie Days of Thunder, he secured a six-year contract with ESPN in 2006. He has also made appearances in movies and reality TV series. He promotes a number of products as a brand ambassador.
