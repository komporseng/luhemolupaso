Sofia Wylie- Professional Career

In 2011, Wylie made her broadcast debut as a dancer on the reality show So You Think You Can Dance. She then participated in America’s Got Talent in 2015, and she also performed on Justin Bieber’s Purpose World Tour around the same time. Sofia appeared in episodes of the Nicky, Ricky, Dicky & Dawn comedy series in 2017. Along with dancers from Utah, she was also cast in the lead role of Buff Driscoll in Andi Mack. She continued to appear in the series till 2019 when she was nominated for a Young Entertainer Award for Best Young Ensemble in a Television Series. Wylie has cast as Gina Porter in the Disney + series High School Musical: The Musical: The Series in February 2019, and the show premiered in November of that year.

Sofia also founded her own production company, AIFOS, in March 2019, and their first project is to adapt Jenny Torres Sanchez’s novel The Fall of Innocence. In June 2019, she also inked a contract with United Talent Agency. Following that, she took two voice roles in 2019, one as Ironheart / Riri Williams in Marvel Rising: Heart of Iron and another as the same character in Marvel Rising: Battle of the Bands. She also played Amelia Mia Brooks in the Disney Channel’s short-form web series Shook, and she appeared in the Australian-American film Back of the Net.
